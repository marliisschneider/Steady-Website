Snacks that don't spike you.

Snacks get a bad reputation they mostly don't deserve. The problem isn't snacking — it's the naked carb snack that spikes your blood sugar, drops you into a crash, and leaves you hungrier than before. Fix the formula and a snack becomes what it should be: steady fuel between meals.

Why the usual snack backfires

The classic afternoon snack — a granola bar, a handful of crackers, a piece of fruit on its own — is a fast, lone carbohydrate. It spikes your blood sugar, which feels like a lift for twenty minutes, then drops you into a crash that leaves you foggy and reaching for more. That's the mechanism behind the mid-afternoon sugar hunt. The snack didn't fail you; the kind of snack did.

The formula: protein or fat, plus fiber

Here's the entire trick, and it fits on a sticky note: pair a protein or a fat with some fiber. That combination digests slowly, flattens the spike, and actually holds you until the next meal. It's the same principle as never eating a carb naked — you're just applying it to snacks. Get this one habit and most "snacking problems" quietly resolve.

Snacks that get it right

  • Apple or pear with nut butter
  • Plain Greek yogurt with berries
  • Cheese with whole-grain crackers or an apple
  • Hummus with raw vegetables
  • A handful of nuts, or jerky with a piece of fruit
  • Hard-boiled eggs, or edamame

The snacks that spike

The ones to treat with suspicion are the lone fast carbs and the "health halo" packaged snacks: chips, crackers by themselves, most granola and "protein" bars (often candy in disguise — read the label), dried fruit on its own, sweetened yogurts, and anything liquid and sweet. None are evil in a one-off, but as your default afternoon habit they keep the spike-crash cycle spinning.

Do you even need to snack?

Honest answer: maybe not. If your meals are built around protein, fiber, and enough fat, you may find the urge to snack fades on its own — because you're actually full. So snack when you're genuinely hungry, not out of habit, boredom, or a 3pm crash you can fix upstream by building better meals. A snack is a tool, not an obligation.

  • Pair every snack. Protein or fat, plus fiber. Never a lone fast carb.
  • Prep grab-and-go options. Boiled eggs, cut veg and hummus, portioned nuts, cheese. Easy beats ideal-but-absent.
  • Skip the liquid sugar. Sweet drinks are the fastest spike with the least staying power.
  • Watch the "health" snacks. Granola bars, sweetened yogurt, and "protein" bars are often sugar in a wellness costume.
  • Snack from real hunger. Ask whether you're hungry or just bored — half the time, a glass of water and a walk answer it.
Start here today

Upgrade one snack: instead of the crackers or the granola bar alone, add a protein or fat — cheese, nut butter, a boiled egg, a handful of nuts. Notice whether the after-snack crash and the too-soon hunger disappear.
