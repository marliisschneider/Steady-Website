About Steady

I got tired of watching smart people feel terrible for no reason.

You already know how to do your job at a high level. You shouldn't have to become a nutrition scientist to stop feeling foggy by 3pm. That's my job.

Coach photo

Why I do this

I spent years watching capable, driven people — the kind who never miss a deadline — quietly running on caffeine, convenience food, and the belief that feeling bad was just the cost of a busy life. It isn't. Most of the time, it's three fixable things: chronic inflammation, blood sugar swings, and a disrupted gut. Nobody had ever explained it to them plainly.

So that's what Steady is. Not a diet. Not an app that guilt-trips you into logging every almond. A real coaching relationship where we figure out what's actually happening in your body and build changes that survive contact with your real week — the travel, the client dinners, the fridge that's empty by Wednesday.

Why 1:1 works when apps don't

Apps optimize for engagement — streaks, notifications, daily logging. They're built to keep you opening the app, not to make you not need it. That's backwards. A tracking app can't tell you your bloating is stress-driven, not food-driven. It can't reorder your lunch so the pasta stops spiking you. It can't notice you've quietly given up in week three and reach out.

Coaching adapts. When your travel schedule blows up your routine, we adjust the plan instead of you feeling like you failed at it. The goal isn't dependence — it's the opposite. I want you to understand your body well enough that, in a few months, you don't need me anymore.

The Steady approach

No macros spreadsheets. No weekly weigh-ins. No before-and-after photos. We measure success by how you feel, how you sleep, and whether the changes are still there in six months.

Philosophy

Three beliefs run through everything I do:

  • Understanding beats willpower. When you know why a food does what it does, the "right" choice stops feeling like deprivation and starts feeling obvious.
  • Consistency beats perfection. A protocol you follow 80% of the time beats a perfect one you abandon in a month. We build for your actual life, not an ideal one.
  • Food is information, not morality. There are no "cheat" days and nothing to feel guilty about. There's just input and how your body responds to it.
