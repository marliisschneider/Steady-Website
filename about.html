About Steady · Meet your coach

I did everything "right" and still felt terrible. Then I figured out why.

I spent ten years as a professional rower, chasing health harder than almost anyone — and my body still hurt. What finally fixed it wasn't another trend. It was going back to real food. That's what I teach now.

Marliis Schneider · Founder & coach

Where I come from

I rowed competitively for ten years. I represented my national team at the junior, U23, and elite level, and I rowed Division I varsity at USC. When your body is your job, nutrition isn't a hobby — it's infrastructure. I thought I understood it better than almost anyone.

So it made no sense that I felt the way I did. Joint pain. Bloating. A low-grade "off" feeling that never fully lifted, even though I was training like an athlete and eating like — I thought — a saint.

I tried every version of "healthy"

Vegan. Super high protein. Keto. Cutting dairy completely, because I'd heard dairy caused inflammation. I chased every trend that promised to be the clean, optimal, science-backed answer. And through all of it, the joint pain and the bloating stayed. I was doing everything the internet called healthy and my body was quietly telling me it wasn't working.

The turning point

I stopped chasing trends and went all the way back to basics. Whole foods only. Dairy back in — full-fat milk, grass-fed butter, real dairy. Meat, fruit, vegetables. And I cut the two things I'd never questioned: processed food and seed oils. My joint pain and bloating were gone within a month, after years of trying.

What that taught me

One month. After years. That was impossible to ignore. It meant the problem was never that I wasn't disciplined enough — it was that I'd been optimizing the wrong things. So I went deep on the research, and the seed-oil piece kept coming up: the industrial oils in nearly every packaged and restaurant food are a major, under-discussed driver of chronic inflammation. Removing them changed how I felt more than any diet ever had.

Then it got personal in another way. When my grandmother was diagnosed with type 1 diabetes, I threw myself into learning how blood sugar actually works so I could help her — the order you eat your food in, how a short walk after a meal blunts the spike, how much movement matters. What shocked me was how many people, smart and health-conscious people, have no real idea how any of it works. Nobody ever taught them.

Why I coach

I'm still an athlete. I still row, I still compete — not at the top level anymore — and I train almost every day, even now that I'm a mom. All of that knowledge is what keeps me and my family genuinely healthy, not just performing-healthy. And I know exactly what it feels like to believe you're doing everything right while your body says otherwise.

That's why Steady exists. Not to sell you the next trend. To teach you what actually being healthy means — and to build it around the real life you already have.

What I believe

  • Real food beats fancy trends. Butter, milk, meat, fruit, vegetables — the things we're actually built to eat. Most "optimized" diets are just new ways to overcomplicate that.
  • Understanding beats willpower. Once you know why a food does what it does in your body, the right choice stops feeling like deprivation and starts feeling obvious.
  • Consistency beats perfection. A way of eating you keep for years beats a perfect protocol you abandon in a month. We build for your actual week.
