The root causes · Inflammation

The anti-inflammatory foods list.

Cutting seed oils and processed food removes what drives inflammation. This is the other half: the real, everyday foods that actively calm it down — and how to build them into meals you'll actually eat.

First, the big picture: no single "superfood" fixes inflammation. What works is the pattern — mostly whole foods, plenty of color, good fats, and none of the industrial oils. If you haven't yet, start with what to remove: the seed-oil problem. Then add these in.

The list — and why each one works

  • Fatty fish (salmon, sardines, mackerel) — rich in EPA and DHA omega-3s, the fats that directly counteract inflammatory pathways. The most powerful item on the list; aim for two or three servings a week.
  • Extra-virgin olive oil — contains oleocanthal, a compound that acts on the same enzymes as ibuprofen. Your everyday cooking and dressing fat.
  • Berries (blueberries, raspberries, strawberries) — anthocyanins that calm a key inflammatory switch, with barely any blood-sugar cost.
  • Leafy greens (spinach, kale, arugula) — loaded with magnesium and polyphenols; low magnesium is strongly linked to inflammation.
  • Cruciferous vegetables (broccoli, cauliflower, Brussels sprouts) — contain sulforaphane, which supports your body's own anti-inflammatory defenses.
  • Turmeric + black pepper — curcumin suppresses multiple inflammatory markers; the pepper boosts its absorption dramatically. Use them together.
  • Ginger and garlic — long-studied kitchen staples with genuine anti-inflammatory compounds. Cook with them freely.
  • Walnuts and other nuts — a good plant source of omega-3s and shown to lower CRP, a key inflammatory marker.
  • Fermented foods (plain yogurt, kefir, sauerkraut, kimchi) — support a healthy gut, and a calm gut means less system-wide inflammation.
  • Green tea — EGCG is one of the most-studied anti-inflammatory compounds in the diet.
  • Dark chocolate (70%+) — real cocoa is rich in anti-inflammatory flavonoids. A square after a meal, not a whole bar.
The one-line version

Fatty fish, olive oil, berries, greens, and colorful vegetables, seasoned with turmeric, ginger, and garlic — cooked at home so no seed oils sneak in. That's the whole anti-inflammatory diet.

How to actually eat them

A list is useless if it stays a list. Here's how these show up in a normal day:

MealAnti-inflammatory build
BreakfastYogurt (fermented) + berries + walnuts
LunchBig leafy salad + salmon + olive oil & lemon
DinnerChicken with turmeric & garlic + roasted broccoli
DrinksGreen tea; water before coffee
TreatA square of dark chocolate after dinner
Don't overthink it

You don't need every food every day, and you don't need supplements. Pick two or three of these to add each week — most people start with fatty fish, olive oil, and berries — and let the rest fall into place.

What matters more than any single food

Adding anti-inflammatory foods on top of a diet full of seed oils and processed food is like bailing a boat that's still leaking. Remove the drivers first, then add these in, and cook at home so you control the oil. That combination — not any one miracle food — is what actually brings inflammation down.
