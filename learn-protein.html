The root causes · Protein

Protein: the lever most people ignore.

Of everything on your plate, protein is the one busy people under-eat the most — and the one with the biggest return on energy, appetite, and holding onto muscle as you age.

Why protein is the one to get right first

Most nutrition debates are noise. Protein isn't. It's the macronutrient your body can't store and can't fake, and it does three things nothing else does as well: it keeps you full, it protects your muscle, and it barely moves your blood sugar. When someone tells me they're starving two hours after breakfast and hunting for a snack by 11am, the first thing I look at isn't willpower — it's how little protein was on the plate.

The pattern I see constantly

A "healthy" breakfast of oatmeal, fruit, and coffee has almost no protein. It spikes, it crashes, and you're hungry by mid-morning. Add three eggs or a bowl of Greek yogurt and the whole day changes — steadier energy, fewer cravings, less snacking.

How much you actually need

The official minimum — roughly 0.8g per kilogram of bodyweight — is set to prevent deficiency, not to help you thrive. For an active adult who wants to hold onto muscle, most evidence points closer to 1.6 to 2.2 grams per kilogram of bodyweight (about 0.7–1g per pound). In practical terms, that usually means 30–50 grams of protein per meal for most adults.

Why animal protein does the heavy lifting

You can hit your protein target on any diet, but it's simply easier and more complete from animal sources. Meat, fish, eggs, and dairy are "complete" proteins — they contain all nine essential amino acids in the right ratios, and they're dense enough that you don't have to eat an enormous volume to get there. Plant proteins count, but they usually come packaged with far more carbohydrate, so hitting 40 grams from lentils alone means a lot of food. If you eat plants only, lean on a wider mix — legumes, soy, and thoughtful pairing — to cover the gaps.

Three habits that fix low protein

  • Front-load breakfast. This is the meal that's almost always protein-empty. Aim for 30g before you touch coffee — eggs, yogurt, or last night's leftovers. It sets the tone for cravings all day.
  • Anchor first, fill in second. Decide your protein for each meal before anything else, then build the vegetables and starch around it. Most people do the reverse and run out of room.
  • Make protein the default snack. Jerky, cheese, a hard-boiled egg, Greek yogurt. Swapping a carb-only snack for a protein one is one of the highest-leverage changes you can make.
Why it matters more with age

After about 40, your body gets less efficient at turning dietary protein into muscle — a phenomenon called anabolic resistance. That's the opposite of a reason to eat less of it. Protecting muscle now is one of the best predictors of how strong, stable, and independent you'll be decades from now.

You don't need a shaker bottle or a spreadsheet. You need real protein at every meal, starting with breakfast. Get that one thing right and the cravings, the crashes, and the constant snacking tend to quiet down on their own.
