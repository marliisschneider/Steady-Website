Step one · free, 20 minutes

It starts with a conversation, not a checkout button.

The discovery call isn't a sales pitch with a timer on it. It's how we both figure out whether coaching is genuinely the right move for you. Here's what actually happens in those 20 minutes:

👋

You talk, I listen

You tell me what's going on — the 3pm crashes, the bloating, the "I know what to eat, I just can't make it stick." No forms to fill out first. Just the real story.

🔍

I ask the pointed questions

What your mornings look like, how often you eat out, what you've already tried, what your sleep is like. Usually two or three answers tell me where the problem actually lives.

🧭

You leave with direction

Fit or not, I'll tell you what I think is driving how you feel and what I'd look at first. You'll get real value from the call even if we never speak again.

What "a good fit" means: your symptoms are the kind food actually fixes (energy, bloating, cravings, brain fog — not a medical condition that needs a doctor first), you're willing to change a few defaults over three months, and our schedules work. If any of those aren't true, I'll say so honestly and point you somewhere better — a physician, a different specialist, or just the free guides on this site.

Step two · if we're a fit

The 90-minute kickoff: where we find the real problem.

Within the first week, we sit down for a proper deep-dive. This is the longest session of the whole program, and it's where most clients have their first "oh — that's why" moment.

1

Symptoms & history

Everything you're feeling and when it started — energy dips, digestion, sleep, skin, joints, mood. Plus health history and anything your doctor has flagged.

2

Labs, if you have them

Recent bloodwork adds useful signal — fasting glucose, lipids, inflammation markers. Not required. Your symptoms and patterns tell us most of what we need.

3

A real look at your week

Not what you wish you ate — what actually happens. The client dinners, the airport food, the fridge that's empty by Wednesday, the 9pm cereal. Zero judgment; this is the raw material we build with.

4

Your custom protocol

Within a few days you get a clear, one-page plan: the two or three root causes we're going after, the specific changes for weeks one and two, and the why behind each one. Not a meal plan — a plan for your life.

The three-month journey

Six calls, three phases, one arc.

We meet every two weeks over video, and you have me on voice and text between calls. Here's what each stretch of the program is actually for:

  1. Month 1 · Reset · calls 1–2

    Strip out what's working against you

    We start with the swaps that pay off fastest: cooking oils, the worst of the ultra-processed food, and real protein at breakfast. On our calls we troubleshoot what's hard, not what's easy — the travel week, the deadline crunch. Most people feel the 3pm crash soften somewhere in weeks 2–4, and that early win is what makes the rest stick.

  2. Month 2 · Rebuild · calls 3–4

    Dial in blood sugar, protein, and your playbooks

    Now we build the systems: meal timing and food order to keep blood sugar steady, protein targets you can actually hit, and your personal restaurant, travel, and grocery playbooks. This is also where I explain the most — you'll understand why each change works, because understanding is what makes it permanent. By the end of month two, the right choices stop taking willpower.

  3. Month 3 · Sustain · calls 5–6

    Make it automatic and life-proof

    We stress-test everything against real life — vacations, holidays, the brutal work quarter — and simplify anything that still takes effort. The last call is a handoff: what to keep doing, what to watch for, and how to self-correct when life knocks you off course. The goal of month three is that you genuinely don't need me anymore.

Between the calls

The coaching doesn't pause for two weeks.

Most of the real change happens between calls — which is why you're never left alone with it. A typical week looks like this:

💬

Unlimited voice & text

Standing in a grocery aisle? At a restaurant with a menu full of landmines? Send a message. I reply within a day, usually much faster.

📸

Real-time troubleshooting

Snap a photo of the label, the menu, or the room-service card. You get an answer for this situation — which quietly teaches you to handle the next one yourself.

🔄

Adjustments as life happens

Surprise trip? Sick kid? Brutal week? The plan flexes instead of breaking. We adjust in real time so a bad week never turns into a lost month.

What we don't do: no daily food logging, no macros spreadsheets, no weigh-ins, no guilt when a week goes sideways. Progress is measured by how you feel, sleep, and function — not by a number on a scale.

The result

What you have when the three months are done.

Every body starts from a different place, so this is the typical arc rather than a guarantee. But for people who do the work, this is what the end looks like:

Steady all-day energy

The 3pm crash is gone or rare, and you're not running on caffeine and willpower to get through the afternoon.

A calmer gut

Less bloating and puffiness, a comfortable middle at the end of the day, and you know which foods were actually the problem.

Cravings that quieted down

With blood sugar steady and protein handled, the constant snacky pull fades on its own — no white-knuckling required.

Playbooks for your real life

Restaurants, airports, hotels, client dinners, the weekly grocery run — you have a default move for each, so none of them derail you.

A kitchen that works for you

A pantry and shopping system where the healthy choice is the easy one, and label-reading takes five seconds instead of five minutes.

The understanding to run it yourself

The real result: you know how your own body works and why each habit matters — so it lasts for years, not until the invoice stops.

Why it's built this way

Most programs fail at the handoff. This one is designed for it.

Rigid plans work until life happens — then they collapse, because you never learned the why. Steady spends three months transferring the thinking to you: every change comes with the reason behind it, every playbook is built for your actual week, and by the last call you're making the adjustments yourself. I'd rather you graduate than renew.
