The basics · Hunger

Why am I always hungry — even after eating?

Hunger an hour after a full meal isn't a discipline problem — it's a signal problem. Your body isn't asking for more food. It's reacting to how the last meal landed.

If you finish lunch and find yourself hunting for a snack by 2pm, you've probably blamed yourself: not enough willpower, no self-control, maybe "just a big appetite." None of that is what's happening. Constant hunger after eating almost always comes down to two mechanical things — and both are fixable this week.

1. Your meals are spiking, then crashing

Meals built on refined carbs — a sandwich, pasta, a grain bowl heavy on the grain — spike your blood sugar fast. Your body responds with a flood of insulin, glucose drops sharply, and that crash reads to your brain as "we need food," even though you just ate. The hunger is real; the need isn't. You're not low on fuel — your fuel gauge is swinging. How the spike-crash cycle works →

2. There wasn't enough protein to register the meal

Protein is what actually tells your body a meal happened. It triggers the satiety hormones that quiet hunger for hours — and most "light" lunches barely contain any. A salad with a sprinkle of chicken is mostly water and carbs; your body files it as a snack. Under 30 grams of protein, many people simply never get the "we're done here" signal. The two-part fix

At your next meal: eat the protein first, and make sure there's real protein to eat — a palm-sized portion or more. Then don't eat carbs naked; pair them with the protein, fat, or fiber already on your plate. The constant background hunger usually goes quiet on its own within days. No willpower required — which is the point.

What it's not: a character flaw

The people who tell me they're "always hungry" are usually the same ones eating the least protein and the most refined carbs — often because they're trying to eat light. The hunger isn't evidence you need more discipline. It's evidence your meals aren't built to hold you. Build them differently and the appetite that felt bottomless turns out to be perfectly reasonable.

When constant hunger is worth a closer look

If you've fixed the protein and the crashes and you're still ravenous — especially alongside energy crashes, stubborn belly fat, and brain fog — it's worth reading about insulin resistance, where hunger signals get progressively louder. And if cravings are your specific flavor of hunger — the 3pm hunt for something sweet — that has its own fix.

This is general education, not medical advice. Sudden, extreme changes in appetite are worth mentioning to your doctor.
