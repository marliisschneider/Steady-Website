Real meals

The food I actually eat.

No sad diet plates, no seed oils, no 40-ingredient recipes. Just simple, whole-food meals that keep your blood sugar steady and your energy even — the same ones I feed my own family. Treat them as templates, not rules.

How to use these

Build every plate the same way.

Protein first, then fiber and fat, and save the starch for last. Cook in butter, ghee, or olive oil — never seed oils. Do that and almost anything becomes blood-sugar-friendly.

Blood-sugar friendly · High protein

The Steady Breakfast Plate

The breakfast that keeps you full until lunch — zero mid-morning crash or snack hunt.

See the recipe

What you need

  • 2–3 pasture-raised eggs
  • 2 slices good-quality bacon
  • ½ avocado
  • A handful of blueberries
  • A small handful of walnuts
  • Butter, for the pan

Method

  1. Fry the eggs in butter to your liking; cook the bacon alongside.
  2. Plate with the avocado, berries, and walnuts.
  3. Coffee after you eat, not instead of eating.
5-minute · Gut-friendly

Overnight Yogurt Bowl

Real dairy, real fiber. The opposite of a sugary "healthy" cereal that spikes you by 10am.

See the recipe

What you need

  • 1 cup full-fat plain yogurt
  • Mixed berries
  • 1 tbsp ground flax or chia
  • 1 tbsp nut butter
  • A small drizzle of honey (optional)
  • A sprinkle of oats or nuts for crunch

Method

  1. Spoon the yogurt into a bowl.
  2. Top with berries, seeds, nut butter, and honey.
  3. That's it — assemble the night before if mornings are chaos.
Real-food fast food

Breakfast Tacos

Everything a drive-thru breakfast wishes it was — made at home, so you control the oil.

See the recipe

What you need

  • 2 corn or sourdough tortillas
  • 2 eggs
  • Leftover roasted potatoes
  • Avocado and a little cheese
  • Salsa, butter for the pan

Method

  1. Soft-scramble the eggs in butter.
  2. Warm the tortillas and the potatoes.
  3. Fill with eggs, potatoes, avocado, cheese, and salsa.
One-pan dinner

Sheet-Pan Chicken & Asparagus

One tray, ten minutes of effort, real protein and vegetables. Weeknight-proof.

See the recipe

What you need

  • Chicken thighs or drumsticks
  • A bunch of asparagus
  • Olive oil, salt, pepper
  • Paprika and garlic
  • A lime

Method

  1. Toss the chicken and asparagus in olive oil and spices.
  2. Roast at 425°F (220°C) for 25–30 min, until the chicken is cooked through.
  3. Finish with a squeeze of lime.
Batch-friendly

30-Minute Real-Food Bolognese

Make a big batch. Real bread and pasta are fine — it's the seed oils and additives that aren't.

See the recipe

What you need

  • 1 lb grass-fed ground beef
  • 1 onion and 2 cloves garlic
  • 1 can crushed tomatoes
  • Olive oil, salt, dried herbs
  • Sourdough or pasta, to serve

Method

  1. Brown the beef with onion and garlic in olive oil.
  2. Add tomatoes and herbs; simmer 20 minutes.
  3. Serve over pasta or with sourdough — vegetables first.
Protein-forward treat

Weekend Waffles That Won't Spike You

Proof that "treat" and "steady" can share a plate. Yogurt and berries instead of a syrup flood.

See the recipe

What you need

  • 2 eggs
  • Oat or almond flour
  • ½ mashed banana
  • Cinnamon and a pinch of salt
  • Full-fat yogurt and berries, to top

Method

  1. Blend the batter until smooth.
  2. Cook in a waffle iron.
  3. Top with yogurt and berries instead of drowning it in syrup.
