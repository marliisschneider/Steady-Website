A 1-week blood-sugar-friendly meal plan.

Seven days of simple, whole-food meals — protein-forward, no seed oils, no calorie counting. Not a rigid diet: a starting template you can swap, repeat, and make your own. Print it and stick it on the fridge.

See the recipes

How to use it

Build every plate the same way: protein and vegetables first, starch or sweet last. Cook in butter, ghee, or olive oil — never seed oils. Take a 10-minute walk after your biggest meal. Do that, and this plan runs itself. Full recipes for the starred meals live on the Real Meals page.

Make it yours

Not hungry for a snack? Skip it. Don't eat pork? Swap the bacon. Vegetarian? Lean on eggs, dairy, and legumes for protein. The structure — protein first, whole foods, no seed oils, a walk after meals — is what matters, not the exact meals.

The one grocery run

Shop the perimeter: eggs, chicken, beef, salmon, full-fat dairy and yogurt, butter, olive oil, a big bag of greens, seasonal vegetables and fruit, potatoes, oats, sourdough, nuts, and dark chocolate. Read the label on anything from the middle aisles. That's the whole list.

This is a general, whole-food meal template — not medical or personalized nutrition advice. If you're managing a condition like diabetes, check with your doctor before making changes.
