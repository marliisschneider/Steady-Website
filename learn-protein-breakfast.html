The playbook · Breakfast

High-protein breakfast ideas (30g, fast).

A high-protein breakfast is the single easiest change with the biggest payoff — steadier energy, fewer cravings, and no mid-morning crash. Here are real, fast ideas that hit 30 grams, no protein powder required.

Why 30 grams, and why breakfast

Most "healthy" breakfasts — oatmeal, toast, fruit, a muffin — are almost pure carbohydrate. They spike your blood sugar, crash you by mid-morning, and leave you hunting for a snack. Front-loading around 30 grams of protein flattens that curve, keeps you full for hours, and sets the tone for steadier energy and fewer cravings all day. It's the highest-leverage meal to get right. The mindset shift

Breakfast doesn't have to be "breakfast food." Some of the best high-protein mornings are just last night's leftovers. Steak and vegetables at 7am beats a bagel every time.

Tips to make it automatic

  • Go savory over sweet. Sweet breakfasts spike hardest. Eggs, cheese, meat, and yogurt keep you level.
  • Cook a batch. Hard-boil a dozen eggs on Sunday; keep cooked meat and cottage cheese on hand for two-minute mornings.
  • Don't fear the fat. Butter, whole eggs, full-fat yogurt — the fat keeps you full and helps you absorb nutrients.
  • Coffee after, not instead. Eat first. Caffeine on an empty stomach makes the crash worse.
  • If you must have carbs, put them after the protein — sourdough with the eggs, not instead of them.
Try it for three days

Swap your usual breakfast for one of the above for three mornings. Most people are genuinely surprised how much steadier the whole day feels — and how the 11am snack craving just disappears.

You don't need a blender full of powder or a complicated recipe. You need protein, first thing, most days. Get that one meal right and a lot of the rest falls into place.
