## About page (about.html)

**Eyebrow:** ABOUT

**Headline:**
Nutrition coaching for people who don't want to think about food all day.

**The story (3 short paragraphs):**

I built Steady after years of trying to eat "right" during travel-heavy weeks, client dinners, and late nights. Diet apps demanded logging. Meal plans assumed a Sunday I didn't have. Nothing worked because nothing was designed for the life I was actually living.

The turning point wasn't willpower — it was understanding. Once I learned what was actually happening in my body (blood sugar spikes, chronic low-grade inflammation, gut disruption from processed food), the fixes became obvious. And they were small. Eat in a certain order. Move for 10 minutes after meals. Swap the oils. Sleep like it matters.

Steady is what I wish I'd had. It's coaching for people who don't want a lifestyle overhaul — they want to feel better while keeping the life they've built.

## Visual direction

**Colors:**
- Deep forest green (primary — evokes health without cliché)
- Cream / off-white (background)
- Warm neutrals (accents)
- Optional accent: soft coral for CTA buttons

**Fonts:**
- Display: Fraunces (or similar warm serif) — for headlines, feels editorial not clinical
- Body: Inter (or similar clean sans) — for readability

**Style principles:**
- Lots of white space
- Confident typography
- No diet culture imagery (no salads, no scales, no before/after)
- Real food photography if any (whole ingredients, market shots — not plated meals)
- Editorial feel — like the best health article you've ever read, not a landing page

**Reference sites for style:**
- www.paperless-post.com (editorial)
- www.mealime.com (calm nutrition brand — but less "app" energy)
- Any Substack of a food/health writer (long-form editorial + trust)

**Mobile-first.** Every page should feel great on a phone.
