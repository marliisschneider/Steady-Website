The Anti-Inflammatory Grocery Guide.

Most of what quietly inflames you comes from a handful of ingredients hiding in "healthy" food. This is the one-page cheat sheet: what to spot, what to swap, and what to actually buy. Print it and take it to the store.

The 10-second rule

You don't need to memorize any of this. You need one habit: turn the package over before it goes in the cart. If the ingredient list includes an industrial seed oil or a string of things you can't picture in nature, put it back. That single move removes most of the damage — because the problem was never real food. It's what got added to it.

Why seed oils

Canola, soybean, sunflower, corn, and their cousins are extremely high in omega-6 fat. Heated and processed, they oxidize and drive chronic, low-grade inflammation — the kind behind stiffness, puffiness, and that vaguely "off" feeling. They're in most packaged and restaurant food. Cutting them is the highest-leverage change you can make.

1 · Put it back — seed oils to spot on labels

If you see any of these in the ingredients, choose something else:

  • Canola oil (aka rapeseed oil)
  • Soybean oil — the most common one; it's in almost everything
  • Vegetable oil — a vague label that's usually soybean or a blend
  • Sunflower & safflower oil
  • Corn oil
  • Cottonseed & grapeseed oil
  • "Partially hydrogenated" anything — trans fats

Common hiding spots: chips, crackers, mayo, salad dressing, hummus, granola bars, bread, roasted nuts, plant-based meat, and most "healthy" packaged snacks.

3 · The whole-food shopping list

Fill your cart from these and the rest takes care of itself. Buy the best quality you can — grass-fed, pasture-raised, and organic where it matters most.

Protein

  • Eggs (pasture-raised)
  • Chicken & turkey
  • Beef & lamb (grass-fed)
  • Salmon & sardines
  • Wild shrimp

Fats

  • Grass-fed butter & ghee
  • Extra-virgin olive oil
  • Avocado & avocado oil
  • Coconut oil
  • Olives

Dairy

  • Full-fat milk
  • Plain full-fat yogurt
  • Real cheese
  • Cottage cheese
  • Kefir

Produce

  • Leafy greens
  • Berries
  • Broccoli, peppers, carrots
  • Avocados & tomatoes
  • Whatever's in season

Smart carbs

  • Potatoes & sweet potatoes
  • White rice
  • Oats
  • Sourdough bread
  • Fruit

Pantry & extras

  • Raw nuts & seeds
  • Honey & maple syrup
  • Sea salt, herbs, spices
  • Vinegars & mustard
  • Dark chocolate (70%+)

4 · Foods that actively fight inflammation

  • Fatty fish (salmon, sardines) — omega-3s that directly counter inflammatory pathways.
  • Extra-virgin olive oil — contains oleocanthal, which works on the same enzymes as ibuprofen.
  • Berries — anthocyanins calm a key inflammatory switch.
  • Leafy greens — the best source of magnesium, which most people are low in.
  • Turmeric + black pepper — curcumin suppresses inflammatory markers; pepper boosts absorption.

Your first week

Start here — don't overhaul everything

1. Replace your cooking oil with butter, ghee, or olive oil.
2. Make one seed-oil swap on a food you buy every week (dressing or mayo is easy).
3. Add 30g of protein to breakfast.
4. Walk 10 minutes after your biggest meal.
That's it. Four changes, kept all week, beat a perfect plan you quit by Sunday.
