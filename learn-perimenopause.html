Nutrition in your 40s: eating through perimenopause.

If the same food and workouts that kept you lean and energized at 30 suddenly stopped working in your 40s — you're not doing it wrong, and you're not imagining it. Your body genuinely changed. Here's what's happening and how to eat for the body you have now.

Important

This is general nutrition education, not medical advice. Perimenopause affects everyone differently, and options like hormone therapy are worth discussing with your doctor. Use this to eat and move smarter — alongside your healthcare provider, not instead of them.

What actually changes

Perimenopause — the years leading up to menopause, often starting in your early-to-mid 40s — is a hormonal shift, not a willpower problem. As estrogen and progesterone fluctuate and decline, a few things change at once:

  • Insulin sensitivity drops. The same carbs spike your blood sugar more than they used to, so the crashes, cravings, and midsection weight get easier to gain.
  • Muscle loss speeds up. Falling estrogen accelerates the natural muscle loss of aging — and muscle is what keeps your metabolism and blood sugar in check.
  • Sleep and mood get shakier. Which makes cravings and stress eating harder to manage, in a loop.
  • Bone loss accelerates. Estrogen protects bone, so this is the window to protect yours.

Why the old approach backfires

The instinct is to eat less and do more cardio — the thing that worked at 30. But cutting calories hard while you're already losing muscle makes it worse: you lose more muscle, your metabolism drops further, and you feel exhausted. The move now isn't less food. It's smarter, stronger food.

What actually helps

  • Eat more protein, not less. Your body gets less efficient at building muscle with age, so protein needs go up, not down. Aim for 30g at each meal, starting with breakfast. This is the single most important shift. How much you need →
  • Lift weights. Strength training twice a week is non-negotiable in your 40s — it protects muscle, bone, blood sugar, and mood all at once. Cardio alone won't do it.
  • Steady your blood sugar. With insulin sensitivity dropping, eating in order and walking after meals matter even more than before. The basics →
  • Prioritize whole food and cut seed oils. Inflammation climbs in these years; real food and fewer industrial oils help calm it. Why seed oils →
  • Mind bone-building nutrients. Protein, calcium, and vitamins D and K2 — full-fat dairy is an easy source of several at once. On dairy →
  • Watch the alcohol. It hits sleep, hot flashes, and blood sugar harder than it used to. You don't have to quit — just notice the trade.
  • Protect your sleep. Poor sleep drives next-day cravings and stress. Steady blood sugar and enough protein actually help you sleep better, too.
The one-line version

In your 40s, stop trying to eat less and start eating stronger: more protein, lift weights, steady your blood sugar, and cut the seed oils. It works with your changing body instead of against it.

You're not broken

So many capable women hit their 40s, feel their body stop responding to everything that used to work, and quietly assume they've failed. You haven't. The rules changed, and no one handed you the new ones. Adjust the inputs to match the body you have now, and steady energy — and strength — come back.

General education only, not medical advice. Talk with your doctor about perimenopause symptoms, bloodwork, and whether options like hormone therapy are right for you.
