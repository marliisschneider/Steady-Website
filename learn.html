The throughline

A few principles run through everything here.

Real food first

If your great-grandmother wouldn't recognize it as food, treat it with suspicion. Whole foods aren't a diet — they're the default your body was built for.

Read the label

Turn the package over before it goes in the cart. Seed oils and additives hide in food marketed as healthy. Ten seconds of reading is the whole game.

Flatten the spikes

Steady energy comes from steady blood sugar. Eat protein and fiber first, save the starch for last, and walk for ten minutes after.

Fat isn't the enemy

Grass-fed butter, full-fat dairy, olive oil, fatty fish. The stable, natural fats keep you full and calm — it's the industrial oils that cause trouble.

Move after you eat

You don't need a gym. A short walk after meals does more for your blood sugar than almost anything you can buy.

Consistency over perfection

What you do most days matters far more than any single meal. Build something you can keep for years, not for a month.
